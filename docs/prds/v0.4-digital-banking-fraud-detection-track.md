# PRD: Banking Fraud Detection Lab v0.4

Status: draft for human review.

## Problem Statement

The curriculum also needs a deeper **Digital-banking fraud detection** track.
v0.1 includes a NovaBank Digital **Scam-to-mule flow** baseline, but the full
scope requires richer session, device, beneficiary, payment, account takeover,
onboarding abuse, and noisy-outcome examples. Learners need to understand how
**Client**, **User**, sessions, devices, beneficiaries, accounts, alerts, and
cases connect in a high-volume digital setting.

The risk is that digital banking becomes a disconnected telemetry project or
that **Client** and **User** are conflated.

## Solution

Deliver a NovaBank Digital track that extends v0.2 foundations and mirrors the
track depth of v0.3. The release should expand scam-to-mule analysis, add account
takeover and suspicious beneficiary-change scenarios, and teach user-centric
feature extraction, threshold tuning, alert triage, and case interpretation.

v0.4 does not replace the v0.1 `02_digital_scam_to_mule` baseline. It adds the
full `05_digital_session_and_payment_fraud` module that builds on that baseline
and turns it into a deeper digital-banking track.

## User Stories

1. As a learner, I want NovaBank Digital scenarios, so that digital-banking fraud detection has a consistent fictional setting.
2. As a learner, I want **Client** and **User** to remain separate, so that legal customer identity and login identity are not confused.
3. As a learner, I want device, session, authentication, IP, geolocation, ASN, VPN, and channel fields, so that digital behavior can be modeled.
4. As a learner, I want beneficiary-change and payment events, so that scam and mule patterns have a realistic event trail.
5. As a learner, I want account takeover examples, so that unusual session and payment behavior can be detected.
6. As a learner, I want onboarding-abuse and early-life account examples, so that mule-account behavior is visible.
7. As a learner, I want SQL features for session risk, beneficiary novelty, velocity, account age, shared devices, and pass-through behavior, so that signals are explainable.
8. As a learner, I want alert-triage examples with noisy outcomes, so that confirmed fraud is not treated as a perfect label.
9. As a learner, I want to compare digital and private-banking **Detection patterns**, so that I understand why feature choices differ.
10. As a learner, I want case notes for APP scams, mule behavior, online-bank control failures, and payment guidance, so that the track connects to public-source learning anchors.
11. As a maintainer, I want digital generation to remain deterministic and bounded, so that notebooks stay runnable locally.
12. As a reviewer, I want NovaBank Digital additions to reuse the shared **Alert lifecycle**, so that track semantics stay coherent.

## Implementation Decisions

- Build v0.4 as a sibling track to v0.3, both depending on v0.2.
- Import the v0.3 track convention contract for feature naming, notebook layout,
  **Detection pattern** metadata, evaluation output format, and case/regulatory
  link structure.
- Center **User** behavior while preserving links to **Client**, **Partner**, **Banking relationship**, accounts, alerts, and cases.
- Add digital **Detection pattern** metadata using conventions established by v0.3.
- Expand the **Scam-to-mule flow** and add account takeover, onboarding abuse, and suspicious beneficiary-change settings.
- Keep digital telemetry synthetic, coarse, deterministic, and explainable.
- Add notebooks for digital feature engineering, account takeover detection, and alert triage.
- Keep streaming and production infrastructure out of scope.

## Testing Decisions

- Test deterministic digital scenario generation and prevalence ranges.
- Test **Client** to **User** mappings, session-to-user mappings, beneficiary-to-client mappings, and alert/case references.
- Test digital feature calculations for session risk, beneficiary novelty, payment velocity, account age, shared devices, pass-through behavior, and risky channel patterns.
- Test SQL examples against SQLite.
- Test that digital metadata and notebook structure follow the shared track
  convention contract.
- Smoke-test digital notebooks on tiny data.
- Regression-test v0.2 foundations and v0.3 private-banking notebooks after digital additions.

## Out Of Scope

- Private-banking track expansion except shared bug fixes.
- Mandatory streaming, Kafka, Spark, or production services.
- Real device fingerprinting or real customer telemetry.
- Full payment-dispute operations or legal reimbursement workflows.
- Graph analytics beyond feature inputs needed later.

## Cross-Version Links

- v0.4 consumes v0.2 foundation hardening and v0.3 track conventions.
- v0.4 adds `05_digital_session_and_payment_fraud` on top of the v0.1
  `02_digital_scam_to_mule` baseline.
- v0.5 should reuse digital case and regulatory hooks.
- v0.6 should use shared devices, beneficiaries, accounts, and pass-through behavior as graph inputs.
- v0.7 should explain digital model outputs and threshold decisions.
- v0.8 should monitor digital alert volume, drift, and queue behavior.
